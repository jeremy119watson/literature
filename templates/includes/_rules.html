Rules

  • The game is played with a regular deck of playing cards, excluding the 2 and Joker cards.

  • The 48 cards are distributed evenly among the two teams of three players each.

  • The objective of the game is to collect all of the cards in an lower suit (from 3 to 8) or an upper suit (from 9 to A).

  • When a player gets all the cards in a lower suit (3 to 8) or an upper suit (9 to A) in a particular suit, he/she declares the completion of the lower/upper suit and the team gets 1 point (for lower suits) or 2 points (for upper suits).

  • The game ends when all of the lower and upper suits are collected.

A player's turn is as follows:

  • The player can ask for a particular card from one of their opponents; the player needs to identify the opponent and ask for a particular card (suit and value - example: 6 of spades).

    • If the called opponent has that card, the opponent must give the card to the player and the current player gets another turn.

    • If the opponent does not have the card, then the current player loses their turn and the turn will now shift to the opponent who was called.

  • If the player collects all the cards in a lower or upper suit in a particular suit, he/she declares the completion of the lower or upper suit.

It is inevitable that two or more opposing players will have a difference of opinion. To settle the matter, the players may:

  • Play rock-paper-scissors

  • Flip a coin

  • Fight until submission
